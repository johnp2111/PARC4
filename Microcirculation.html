What is Microcirculation?

Microcirculation refers to the flow of blood through the smallest blood vessels in the body: capillaries, arterioles, and venules. These vessels are responsible for delivering oxygen and nutrients to tissues and removing waste products.

Why Is It Important?

  • Supports tissue health and healing
  • Helps regulate blood pressure
  • Plays a role in inflammation and immune response
  • Vital for organ function and general well-being

How to Improve Microcirculation

  1. Regular physical activity
  2. Healthy diet rich in antioxidants
  3. Hydration and good sleep
  4. Managing stress
  5. Use of compression garments or therapy as advised
